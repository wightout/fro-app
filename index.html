§ 2

What it does, and what it doesn't

Scope

It does 07

  • Logs jobs after you finish them — aircraft, system, what you used, what you learned
  • Keeps your tool inventory: condition, location, calibration dates, who borrowed what
  • Searches every job and tool you've ever logged. Fuzzy matching, no typing perfect
  • Generates printable PDF reports for the binder or the toolbox
  • Exports single jobs or tool kits for AirDrop to a teammate
  • Tracks time on task with a punch clock — actual hours, not guessed hours
  • Runs fully offline. Hangars, flight lines, bays with no signal

It doesn't 06

  • Replace Technical Manuals, Work Cards, or approved procedures
  • Sign off on anything. There are no approvals, audit trails, or QA workflows
  • Sync to a cloud. Your data stays on your device
  • Need an account or login
  • Collect telemetry, analytics, or usage data of any kind
  • Charge a subscription. $2.99 once, that's the whole transaction
§ 3

Sticky notes, made searchable

Premise

Every TM had them. Yellow stickies, pencil margins, dog-eared pages with arrows. The real knowledge — the stuff that wasn't in the procedure but mattered every time you ran it.

Sticky · TM pg 4-12
“Don't use this socket — it rounds the head. Try the deep 12pt.”
Margin · TM pg 7-3
“Ground strap goes here, not where the diagram shows.”
Tab · TM pg 11-9
“Use the alternate hazmat. Spec one ate the seal twice.”
Pencil · TM pg 3-7
“Torque to 32 ft-lb here. Spec calls for 28 — strips threads on this airframe.”

Digital manuals don't have margins. PDFs don't take stickies. The shared knowledge that used to live in those notes is gone — or sitting in someone's locker, on the back of a work card, only useful to one person.

FRO is the format those notes deserved all along. Searchable — by aircraft, system, or the word you can almost remember. Readable — your voice, your structure, six months or six years later. Shareable — AirDrop a job to the new mechanic on the line, or trade the whole library with a teammate. The sticky note finally gets a system around it.

§ 5

The loop

Retrospective workflow
01

Walk in prepared

Search prior jobs on this airframe and system. Pull what you used last time and what to watch for.

02

Run the procedure

The TM, AMM, and Work Card stay in charge. FRO doesn't sign off, doesn't approve, doesn't get in the way.

03

Finish the job

The TM tells you the procedure. FRO captures what isn't in the TM — the socket that fit, the alternate that worked.

04

Log it

Aircraft, system, what you used, what to remember. Two minutes if you're slow.

05

Search next time

Three months or three years later, the same airframe rolls in. The notebook is already open.
